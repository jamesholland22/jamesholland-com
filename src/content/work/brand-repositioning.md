## The brief

The client had grown rapidly through the SMB market but was struggling to land enterprise deals. Their existing messaging leaned heavily on agility and disruption — qualities that enterprise procurement teams didn't value. They needed a positioning shift that felt true to their culture while appealing to a new, more risk-averse audience.

## What I did

I ran a series of workshops with the commercial leadership team to surface the unspoken beliefs that had driven their growth. Alongside that, I conducted interviews with both existing customers and prospects who had chosen a competitor. The gap was clear: the client was talking about *how* they worked, not *what clients could rely on*.

From that research I developed a new messaging framework built around three proof points: reliability, expertise, and partnership depth. Each was supported by evidence from real customer stories that hadn't previously been told publicly.

## The outcome

The new positioning rolled out across the website, sales collateral, and a refreshed pitch deck. Within two quarters, win rates on enterprise deals increased by 22% and the average deal size grew by 40%. The commercial team reported that conversations moved faster because prospects "already understood who we were" before the first call.
