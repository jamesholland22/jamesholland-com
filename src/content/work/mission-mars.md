## The brief

Microsoft needed to create hands-on developer training that would actually get developers excited to show up. Standard tutorial formats — static documentation, webinars, slide decks — weren't cutting through. The ask was to create something inspiring, immersive, and genuinely fun, without sacrificing technical rigour.

## What I did

We created *Mission Mars: Fourth Horizon* — an interactive training series built around an original narrative. The setting: a near future in which humans have colonised Mars. The mission: a last-ditch effort to reconnect stranded Martian astronauts with Earth, using Microsoft tools and technologies to solve a series of escalating technical crises.

Developers weren't just running through exercises. They were enlisted into a mission. Each technical challenge was framed as a story beat, with real stakes and a reason to care about the outcome. The narrative gave the training momentum and made the Microsoft tools feel purposeful rather than arbitrary.

We ran the programme as a global series of workshops, bringing the experience to developer communities across 31 countries.

## The results

- **5,000 developers** trained across **31 countries**
- **59 workshops** totalling nearly **1,000 hours** of training activity
- Among the **highest satisfaction scores in Microsoft's history** for a training programme
- The team was invited by **NASA** to run a special session at the Johnson Space Center in Houston — a mark of the programme's credibility with the scientific and developer community
