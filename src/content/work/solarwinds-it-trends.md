## The challenge

SolarWinds' IT Trends Index is a valuable annual report on the state of the IT industry — full of data that IT professionals, analysts, and journalists genuinely care about. But the format was doing the content a disservice. A PDF is something you download, skim, and forget. SolarWinds needed a way to make the data worth coming back to, and to give journalists and IT pros a tool they could use rather than just quote.

## What I did

Over three years, we pushed the IT Trends Index progressively further into interactive territory — building tools that let IT professionals dig directly into the data, filter by role, company size, or region, and use the findings to inform their own planning.

The key insight was that journalists and analysts don't just want to read research. They want to pull their own angles from it. An interactive tool lets them do that — and several went further, embedding the full experience directly in their coverage. That's something a static PDF can never achieve.

Each year we raised the bar. From initial interactive data visualisations, we moved toward narrative-driven experiences that walked users through the most significant findings while giving them freedom to explore further.

## The results

- **5x more media coverage** than static report assets — consistently, year over year
- Multiple publications **embedded the full interactive experience** in their editorial coverage
- Three consecutive years of the programme, each iteration more sophisticated than the last
- Established interactive research assets as a standard format for SolarWinds' broader content strategy
