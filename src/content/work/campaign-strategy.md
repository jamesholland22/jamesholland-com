## The brief

A growing SaaS company was launching their most significant product update in three years. They had a great product story but no clear plan for how to build anticipation before launch or sustain momentum afterward. My job was to create a campaign architecture that their internal team could execute confidently.

## What I did

Starting from the product's core value proposition, I mapped out a three-phase campaign: awareness, consideration and conversion. For each phase I defined the audience, message priority, channel mix, and success metrics. I also wrote the creative brief that shaped the campaign's visual and tonal direction.

A key decision was to lead with customer outcomes rather than product features — a shift that made the campaign more relatable and shareable, especially on LinkedIn where much of their audience lived.

## The outcome

The launch campaign generated 2.4M impressions in the first two weeks. Organic engagement on LinkedIn outperformed the previous year's best-performing post by 4x. The email nurture sequence — built around the messaging framework I developed — achieved a 38% open rate, compared to their industry benchmark of 22%.

The internal team said it was the most clearly briefed campaign they'd ever run.
