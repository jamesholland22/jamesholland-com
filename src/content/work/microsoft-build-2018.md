## The challenge

Microsoft Build is the company's flagship developer conference — the Super Bowl of the Windows ecosystem. But in-person attendance is capped at just 6,500 people. For a platform trying to reach millions of developers worldwide, that's a significant constraint. Our job was to extend the event's reach to the largest possible online audience and drive real consideration for Microsoft tools and technologies — all in real time, over five days.

## What I did

The programme had two interlocking parts.

**Real-time content production.** We built an on-the-ground team to capture, edit, and publish keynote highlights and session clips as they happened. Speed was everything — a clip published within minutes of a keynote moment drives exponentially more engagement than the same clip published hours later. Over five days we produced and promoted 280 pieces of content.

**Build MCs — the influencer layer.** With over 200 individual sessions, even the most dedicated developer couldn't keep up with everything. We partnered with influential developers, each specialising in a different area of the platform, to give our audience curated, credible guides to the conference. By using separate influencers for specific developer types — front-end, systems, game dev, cloud — we built a much more precise audience strategy than a single generalist voice could deliver. The #BuildMC programme drove 174,772 social engagements on its own, plus significant halo effects in video views and web traffic. Influencer involvement also put 70 more videos on the ground year-over-year.

## The results

- **3 million social video views** during the event window
- **285% increase in web traffic** driven by engagement retargeting post-event
- **45,000 social shares** across 280 pieces of content in five days
- **75% reduction in online RSVP cost** using lookalike and custom social audiences
- **174,772 social engagements** from the #BuildMC influencer programme alone
