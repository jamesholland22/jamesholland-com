## The challenge

Mercedes-Benz was competing for engineering talent in Silicon Valley — one of the most contested recruitment markets in the world. The problem wasn't the opportunity. Mercedes-Benz Research & Development North America was doing genuinely fascinating work at the intersection of automotive engineering and emerging technology. The problem was visibility. The company's existing social channels were dormant, its digital presence was minimal, and it had no content strategy to communicate what working there actually looked like.

Against Google, Apple, and every well-funded startup in the Bay Area, Mercedes-Benz was invisible to the engineers it needed.

## What I did

We developed a content strategy built on a simple principle: show the work, not the brand. Engineers don't respond to corporate messaging. They respond to technical challenges, interesting problems, and evidence that the people around them are doing impressive things.

We relaunched the digital channels with a steady cadence of content highlighting the people and projects behind the Mercedes-Benz engineering operation — the researchers building autonomous systems, the teams pushing the boundaries of connected vehicle technology, the collaboration between Silicon Valley and Stuttgart. We launched a new website and blog to give this content a permanent home, and rebuilt the social channels from scratch to make them worth following.

We paired the owned content strategy with an earned media programme, securing coverage that gave the employer brand third-party credibility beyond what owned channels alone could provide.

## The results

- **11x audience growth** — from 3,000 to more than 35,000 followers in under two years
- **26% of blog visitors** went on to explore career opportunities at Mercedes-Benz
- **90+ pieces of earned media coverage** generated by the content programme
- **Key engineering roles directly attributed** to blog and social media content — measurable hiring impact from a content strategy
