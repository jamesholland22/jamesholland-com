## The challenge

Walmart is the world's largest retailer. That scale is also its biggest liability when it comes to small business audiences.

The launch of Walmart Business — a dedicated B2B commerce platform for small businesses and nonprofit organizations — needed to do two things simultaneously: cut through the parent brand's consumer shadow and actively counter a deeply ingrained narrative that Walmart hurts small businesses, not helps them.

The goal wasn't just awareness. It was trust. And not just with SMB customers, but with Walmart's own leadership — proving that a B2B-focused brand could establish real credibility in a crowded space.

## The approach

The strategy was built around six interconnected pillars, designed to reach small businesses across every stage of the funnel — from first awareness to loyal customer.

**Executive thought leadership.** We positioned Ashley Hubka, SVP and General Manager of Walmart Business, as a genuine champion for small businesses — not a corporate spokesperson. Through earned media placements, a social platform presence, and contributed content (including a bylined column in *Entrepreneur*), we built a visible, credible voice at the top of the brand.

**Earned media.** We secured features in the outlets that matter to business decision-makers: *Forbes*, *Fortune*, *Bloomberg*, *Small Business Currents*, and regional *Business Journals* across the country.

**Branded content hub.** We launched a content destination built specifically for SMB audiences — a hub covering the topics and practical insights small business owners actually search for. Over 250 pieces of original content gave the brand a reason to be bookmarked, not just visited once.

**Social presence.** We built Walmart Business's social presence from zero, growing to 4,000+ followers across platforms in the first ten months.

**Branded events.** We stood up an events programme including webinars and in-person salon dinners, giving the brand a live presence in the SMB community and generating direct relationship opportunities.

**Lifecycle marketing.** We developed three-plus lifecycle campaigns covering prospect nurture, customer acquisition, and retention — built around the Walmart Business brand promise of saving time, money, and hassle.

## The results

The lifecycle campaigns delivered the most measurable impact:

- **800% increase in GMV** driven through lifecycle campaign activity
- **16,000+ customers** converted directly from prospect nurture sequences
- **40% reduction** in email unsubscribe rates against benchmark
- **6.5% average CTOR** across all email campaigns — compared to a 0.07% CTOR benchmark for Walmart's B2C programs

Beyond the numbers, the programme established Walmart Business as a recognised focus within Walmart's broader eCommerce organisational goals — with the parent brand actively beginning to cross-promote and drive audiences toward the B2B platform. The brand awareness, offering credibility, and content infrastructure built during this period laid the foundation for Walmart Business's continued growth.
