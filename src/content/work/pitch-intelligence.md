## The problem

Agency new business teams spend hours researching prospects before a pitch — but the output is inconsistent and rarely structured in a way that's useful for strategy. I wanted to see whether that research could be systematised.

## What I built

A simple tool that takes a prospect's name and URL, then pulls together a structured brief: category context, brand positioning signals, recent campaigns, key messages, and observable gaps. The output is formatted for easy sharing and is designed to prompt strategic questions rather than just report facts.

## The result

Used internally across a handful of pitches. Teams reported that it saved 2–3 hours of prep time per pitch and produced more consistent strategic starting points. Currently exploring whether it's worth building out further.
